Tyson "Tenz" Ngo

Early Life:
Tyson Ngo, known by his gamer tag "TenZ," was born on May 5, 2001, in Nanaimo, on Vancouver Island, British Columbia. From a young age, he exhibited a passion for gaming, particularly first-person shooters. He began his gaming journey with titles like "Counter-Strike: Global Offensive" (CS:GO), where he honed his skills and developed a reputation as a talented player.
Rise to Fame:
TenZ's professional career began in 2019 when he joined Cloud9 (C9) as a CS:GO player. His exceptional aim and game sense quickly garnered attention in the esports community. However, it was his transition to "Valorant," Riot Games' tactical shooter released in 2020, that truly launched his career.

In Valorant, TenZ quickly became known for his mechanical skill, agility, and ability to perform under pressure. His playstyle and innovative strategies made him a standout player, helping Cloud9 achieve significant success in various tournaments.
Professional Career:
In 2020, TenZ was loaned to the Valorant team of Cloud9, where he continued to impress with his performances. His standout moments included clutch plays and exceptional use of agents, which earned him a reputation as one of the best players in the game.

In 2021, TenZ's career took another leap when he joined Sentinels on a permanent basis after a successful loan period. He became an integral part of the team, helping them secure multiple tournament victories, including the VCT Stage 2 Masters in Reykjavik, which marked a historic achievement as the first international LAN event for Valorant.
Streaming and Content Creation:
In addition to his professional play, TenZ is also a popular content creator and streamer. His engaging personality and entertaining gameplay have attracted a large following on platforms like Twitch and YouTube, where he shares his gaming experiences, tutorials, and highlights.
Legacy and Impact:
TenZ has become an influential figure in the esports community, inspiring many aspiring gamers with his dedication and skill. His contributions to both Valorant and the broader gaming landscape continue to shape the future of competitive gaming.
Personal Life:
Outside of gaming, TenZ enjoys spending time with friends and engaging in various hobbies, including fitness and art. He remains grounded and focused on his goals, both as a player and a content creator.

As of 2023, Tyson "TenZ" Ngo continues to compete at the highest levels of Valorant, solidifying his status as one of the sport's top talents.
